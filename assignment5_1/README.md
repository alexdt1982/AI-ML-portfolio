#Findings

This analysis explored the factors that influence what makes drivers more likely to accept coupons. By comparing different customer segments, several clear behavioral patterns emerged.

##Bar Coupons

Drivers who accepted bar coupons tended to share a few key characteristics. They were more likely to be over the age of 25, traveling without children, and frequent bar visitors (more than three times per month).

Frequent bar visitation was the strongest predictor of acceptance. This suggests that individuals who already incorporate bars into their routine are more responsive to incentives, likely because the coupon aligns with their existing habits and provides an opportunity to reduce spending on activities they already enjoy.

##Coffee House Coupons

For coffee house coupons, acceptance was also linked to consistent behavioral patterns. Drivers who accepted these coupons were more likely to be over 25, employed, and visit coffee houses at least one to three times per month.

This indicates that regular coffee consumers are more inclined to take advantage of discounts, especially when the offer fits naturally into their daily routines.

##Key Takeaways

Across both coupon types, prior behavior (visiting frequency) was the most significant factor influencing acceptance. Demographic and situational factors—such as age, employment status, and whether or not the driver was by themselves or had a passenger in the car—also played a role, but to a lesser extent.

The effect of visiting frequency was particularly strong for bar coupons, where high-frequency visitors were much more likely to accept offers compared to infrequent visitors. While a similar trend existed for coffee house coupons, the difference was less noticeable.
